Success Stories

See how your support is changing lives across South Africa

Thandi Maseko

Thandi grew up in a small village outside Tzaneen with a dream of studying Mechatronic Engineering at the University of Pretoria. Through FundMyFuture, she raised R48,500 to cover her first-semester fees and now works on 3D-printed prosthetic limbs for children.

"FundMyFuture didn't just fund my studies—it restored my belief that people care about my dream."
Aiden Naidoo

Aiden, a passionate coder from Durban, almost deferred his studies after his father lost his job. With the help of FundMyFuture, he raised R36,000 to continue his Computer Science degree and now tutors coding at a local NGO.

"I used to think crowdfunding was just for startups — but it gave me my start."
Nomvelo Khumalo

Nomvelo, a Pharmacy student from Pietermaritzburg, faced exclusion when her funding was delayed. Her FundMyFuture campaign went viral, raising R52,000 in 10 days and allowing her to graduate on time.

"Every donation felt like a hand pulling me forward when I was ready to stop trying."
Liam Jacobs

Liam, a Fine Arts student at Stellenbosch University, couldn't afford materials for his final exhibition. Thanks to FundMyFuture, he raised R18,000 in two weeks and went on to win the Dean's Award.

"The platform didn't just help me finish my degree—it helped me believe that creativity has real value."
Zanele Dlamini

Zanele, a Biotechnology student at UKZN, was researching eco-friendly water filters but lacked lab funds. Her FundMyFuture campaign exceeded its target, raising R57,000, and later won a provincial science award.

"My project was small, but FundMyFuture turned it into something that could help communities across South Africa."
Amy Daniels

Amy, a creative from Port Elizabeth, wanted to become a UX/UI designer but couldn't afford design software or equipment. Her FundMyFuture campaign raised R22,000, funding her tools and studies. She now mentors other design students.

"Every donation was like a vote of confidence in my creativity — I'll never forget that."
Sasha Petersen

Sasha grew up in Mitchells Plain with a lifelong goal to become a doctor serving underprivileged communities. Her FundMyFuture campaign raised R64,000 in 12 days, allowing her to continue her MBChB studies at Stellenbosch University.

"Because of FundMyFuture, I didn't just stay in school — I found a whole community that believes in my purpose."
