OUR STORY

FundMyFuture was born from the simple idea that education is a right, not a privilege. Our founders witnessed countless talented students unable to pursue their studies due to financial constraints, and wanted to create a solution that bridges that gap.

Our platform bridges that gap connecting verified student needs with local donors, turning small contributions into meaningful educational impact.





OUR MISSION

At FundMyFuture, our mission is to make education more accessible and equitable by connecting students in need with community donors who care. Through transparency, trust, and technology, we aim to empower learners, inspire giving, and create lasting change one micro-scholarship at a time.

We aim to build a transparent, trustworthy space where micro-donations create real impact helping students stay in school, complete their studies, and achieve their dreams.

How does FundMyFuture work?

Both students and funders create accounts on the platform to join the FundMyFuture community.
Verified students post their educational needs, which are limited to tuition fees, books and accommodation.
Each request is reviewed and confirmed to ensure legitimacy and fairness
Donors explore verified requests and choose which student to assist based on their story or need.
Contributions are made safely through trusted payment gateways for transparency and protection. Students can also share progress updates, allowing funders to see the tangible results of their generosity and continued impact

Success Stories

Amy Daniels

Amy, a creative from Port Elizabeth, wanted to become a UX/UI designer but couldn’t afford design software or equipment. Her FundMyFuture campaign raised R22,000, funding her tools and studies. She now mentors other design students.

"Every donation was like a vote of confidence in my creativity — I'll never forget that."
Thandi Maseko

Thandi grew up in a small village outside Tzaneen with a dream of studying Mechatronic Engineering at the University of Pretoria. Through FundMyFuture, she raised R48,500 to cover her first-semester fees and now works on 3D-printed prosthetic limbs for children.

"FundMyFuture didn't just fund my studies—it restored my belief that people care about my dream."
Sasha Petersen

Sasha grew up in Mitchells Plain with a lifelong goal to become a doctor serving underprivileged communities. Her FundMyFuture campaign raised R64,000 in 12 days, allowing her to continue her MBChB studies at Stellenbosch University.

"Because of FundMyFuture, I didn't just stay in school — I found a whole community that believes in my purpose."
